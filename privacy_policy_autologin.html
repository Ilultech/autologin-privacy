Privacy Policy

Last updated: October 25, 2025

Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how AutoLogin protects user privacy and handles data. AutoLogin is designed to help users securely autofill login credentials for websites and systems they use.

Data and Privacy Overview

  • AutoLogin operates in a way that ensures your credentials remain secure and private.
  • Usernames and passwords are always encrypted before being transmitted and stored.
  • AutoLogin does not share, sell, or disclose your credentials to third parties.

How AutoLogin Works

AutoLogin accesses your encrypted credentials only when you request autofill for a system you use. The decryption happens securely within the extension on your device. This ensures your sensitive information never leaves your control in plaintext form.

  • All communication between your browser and the storage system is encrypted via secure HTTPS connections.
  • Credentials are encrypted using strong, industry-standard encryption algorithms (AES).
  • The extension only decrypts data when needed for autofill, and it does not store your decrypted credentials outside your device.

Data Collection

AutoLogin does not collect personal data beyond what is necessary to perform autofill functionality. We do not track user activity or monitor the sites you visit.

Third-Party Services

AutoLogin does not use third-party analytics that collect personally identifiable information. Any optional services that interact with your encrypted data are clearly disclosed within the extension.

User Control and Choices

  • You can enable or disable AutoLogin for any site at any time via the extension settings.
  • You can remove or revoke stored credentials at any time using the system interface.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in features, technology, or legal requirements. The "Last updated" date will reflect the most recent revision.
